ABOUT

I focus on building interfaces that are easy to understand from the first interaction. Many digital products feel confusing not because they lack features, but because their structure is unclear. When users have to guess what to do next, the experience becomes unnecessarily difficult.

Because of that, I pay close attention to how information is organized and how flows are structured, so interactions feel natural without requiring extra effort. I am also interested in how interactions between users and businesses can be made clearer and more relevant — not just to optimize metrics, but to genuinely support how they operate in their daily activities.

As often emphasized in UI/UX, “Don’t make me think.” This principle guides my approach: if something feels confusing, there is usually a simpler way to design it.
